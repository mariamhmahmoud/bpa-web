Bureau of Engraving and Printing

Want to see where money comes from? Stop by the U.S. Bureau of Engraving and Printing, where you can learn all about how U.S. currency is printed, and may even get the chance to see money being made!

How to get a tour: Visit the Tour and Visitor Center. Tickets are distributed on a first-come basis. Group tours must be reserved in advance. Tours run every 30 minutes.

Cost: FREE

Hours:

  • Public Tours: 9 a.m. to 10:45 p.m. & 12:30 p.m. to 2:00 p.m. Monday-Friday
  • Group Tours (10-50 visitors): 11:00 a.m. to 12:15 p.m. Monday-Friday
  • Visitor Center Gift Shop (no ticket required) 8:30 a.m. to 2:45 p.m. September-March, 8:30 a.m. to 6:00 p.m. April through August

Security Measures:

  • All visitors must go through a security screening
  • Foods and drinks are prohibited
  • Strollers are not allowed on tour
  • Weapons are prohibited

Address: 301 14th St SW, Washington, DC 20228

Department of State

Throughout a president's career, he or she is tasked with making many decisions on foreign policy issues, international affairs, and so much more. Visit the State Department, home to the president's advisers on such issues.

How to get a tour: To visit the Eighth-floor Diplomatic Reception Rooms, reservations need to be made up to 90 days in advance. Tours are offered Monday-Friday at 9:30 a.m., 10:30 a.m., and 2:45 p.m and are 45 minutes long. In order to enter the building, visitors need to check in at the information desk inside the C Street entrance (be sure to bring photo identification). A Department employee will be with you at all times during your visit.

Cost: FREE

Hours: 8:15 a.m. to 5:00 p.m. Monday-Friday, Closed Saturday-Sunday

Security Measures:

  • Strollers are prohibited
  • Bring photo identification

Address: 2201 C St NW, Washington, DC 20520

More Information Here

Eisenhower Executive Office Building

We often only see the President as the leader of the nation, but there's a lot more going on behind the scenes than meets the eye. Stop by the Eisenhower Executive Office Building to visit the White House staff offices, and learn about the people working backstage in the Executive Branch.

How to get a tour: Since this is part of the White House, you must get a tour of the White House to tour the Eisenhower Executive Office Building.

Cost: FREE

Hours: 24 hours daily

Security Measures: N/A

Address: 1650 Pennsylvania Ave NW, Washington, DC 20502

More Information Here

Federal Bureau of Investigation

Ever wanted a look into the how the United States enforces national security? Visit the U.S. Federal Bureau of Investigation, where you can tour interactive multimedia exhibits, content, and artifacts from real FBI cases. Gain some insight into the FBI's history and operations, and learn about how it protects our nation.

How to get a tour: Tours must be requested through your Congressional Office ahead of time (between 4 weeks-5 months) and all visitors must be U.S. citizens or green card holders.

Cost: FREE

Hours: 9 a.m. to 3 p.m. Monday-Friday, Closed Saturday-Sunday

Security Measures:

  • The following are prohibited:
    • Backpacks, diaper bags, fanny packs, briefcases, luggage, computer and camera bags, or bags and purses larger than a clutch
    • Point-and-shoot cameras, SLR cameras, or video recorders (cell phone cameras are permitted)
    • Strollers
    • Food, beverages, tobacco products, personal grooming items (e.g. makeup, lotion, etc.)
    • Any pointed objects
    • Aerosol containers
    • Guns, ammunition, fireworks, electric stun guns, mace, martial arts weapons/devices, or any knives

Address: 935 Pennsylvania Ave NW, Washington, DC 20535

More Information Here

Georgetown Station Post Office

A beautiful piece of architecture and still functioning post office, The Georgetown Station Post Office is great if you're looking for a relaxing and quick sightseeing adventure. Admire the beautiful stonework and purchase stamps while you're there!

How to get a tour: No tours are offered.

Cost: FREE

Hours: 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. Monday-Saturday, Closed Sunday

Security Measures: N/A

Address: 1215 31st St NW, Georgetown, Washington DC, DC 20007-3423

More Information Here

Library of Congress

Love books? Come visit the Library of Congress, and you'll be visiting the largest library in the entire world. Explore millions of photographs, maps, newspapers, books, and manuscripts from throughout history.

How to get a tour: Group tours of more than 10 people must be reserved in advance online. The Thomas Jefferson: The Man & His Ideas and Music and the Performing Arts exhibits only offer group tours. The Jefferson Building, Baseball Americana, and Echoes of the Great War Tour are offered to groups of any sizes. Tours of groups with less than 10 people are offered on a first come, first served basis. The following times are available tour times:

  • Thomas Jefferson Building:
    • 10:30 a.m., 11:30 a.m., 12:30 p.m., 1:30 p.m., 2:30 p.m., and 3:30 p.m. Monday-Friday
    • 10:30 a.m., 11:30 a.m., 1:30 p.m., 2:30 p.m. Saturday
  • Baseball Americana: 11 a.m. on Wednesdays, Thursdays, and Fridays and 1 p.m. on Saturday
  • Echoes of the Great War: 1 p.m. on Tuesdays, Thursdays, Fridays, and the first Saturday of the month
  • Thomas Jefferson: The Man and His Ideas and Music and the Performing Arts: Available upon request

Cost: FREE

Hours: 8:30 a.m. to 4:30 p.m. Monday-Saturday, Closed Sunday and all major holidays

Security Measures:

  • Must pass through security screening
  • Weapons are prohibited
  • Open food and drink are not allowed in public areas
  • Silence electronics
  • No flash photography

Address: 101 Independence Ave SE, Washington, DC 20540

More Information Here

Sidney Yates Building

Ever wonder where Smokey the Bear works? It's not in the forest, but in the Sidney Yates Building, home to the U.S. Forest Service. Tour the history of the Forest Service, and get the chance to meet Smokey yourself!

How to get a tour: No tours available

Cost: FREE

Hours: 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. Monday-Friday, Closed Saturday & Sunday

Security Measures: N/A

Address: 201 14th St SW, Washington, DC 20227

More Information Here

Supreme Court

Want to see how laws are examined? How about how some of the inner structures of our government operate? Visit the U.S. Supreme Court, where you can tour the home of our Justices, who debate court cases and how the Constitution works.

Cost: FREE

How to get a tour: Court sessions are open to the public. Visitors are able to view court sessions on a first come, first served basis.

Hours: 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. Monday-Friday, Closed Saturday & Sunday

Security Measures:

  • Be prepared to pass through security screening at your point of entry. All items must be screened in x-ray machines and all visitors will be asked to pass through metal detectors.
  • Please tour the building quietly, as working offices are adjacent to public hallways.
  • No photography or audio/video recording is allowed inside the courtroom.
  • Smoking is prohibited inside the building.
  • Consumption of food and beverages is permitted only in the cafeteria and vending machine alcove.
  • Touching of portraits, busts, or other artifacts on exhibit is strictly prohibited.
  • Any visitors unable to maintain proper behavior will be asked to leave by the Supreme Court Police.

Address: 1 First St NE, Washington, DC 20543

More Information Here

The Pentagon

Our government does a lot to protect the nation. If you want to see it for yourself, visit the Pentagon, home of the U.S. Department of Defense. Here, you can learn all about the steps they take to keep the country safe.

How to get a tour: Tours must be booked 14-90 days in advance. Public tours cannot exceed 60 people.

Cost: FREE

Hours: 9 a.m. to 3 p.m. Monday-Friday, Closed Saturday & Sunday

Security Measures:

  • Adults must bring a photo ID
  • No photography allowed
  • Must pass through security screening

Address: Washington, DC 22202

More Information Here

U.S. Capitol

Love politics? Explore the U.S. Capitol, where you can watch how our laws our made, the very basis of our government and see all the beautiful architecture and art scattered throughout the building.

How to get a tour: Reserving a tour in advance is recommended, but it is not required. Same-day passes are available, but there could be a wait to get them. Groups of 15 or more should reserve a tour ahead of time here.

Cost: FREE

Hours: 8:30 a.m. to 4:30 p.m. Monday-Saturday, Closed Sunday, Thanksgiving Day, Christmas Day, New Year's Day, and Inauguration Day

Security Measures:

  • The following are prohibited:
    • Foods and drinks
    • Aerosol containers
    • Non-aerosol spray (Prescriptions for medical needs are permitted.)
    • Any pointed object, e.g. knitting needles and letter openers (Pens and pencils are permitted.)
    • Any bag larger than 18" wide x 14" high x 8.5" deep
    • Weapons

Address: First St SE, Washington, DC 20004

More Information Here

United States Department of the Treasury

Want to learn about how your taxes are used? Schedule a visit for the United States Department of the Treasury, where you can learn about taxes, how U.S. money is used, and more!

How to get a tour: You must register a tour in advance with your Congressional Office.

Cost: FREE

Hours:8 a.m. to 5 p.m. Monday-Friday, Closed Saturday-Sunday

Security Measures:

  • Must have a photo ID, name, social security number, and date of birth at hand to enter
  • No strollers (no storage area onsite)
  • No backpacks (no storage area onsite)
  • No cameras - photography (still or video) is not permitted in the building

Address: 1500 Pennsylvania Ave NW, Washington, DC 20220

More Information Here

White House

Want to see where our President lives? Come see the White House, where you can explore the historic living space for our past presidents since the year 1800. Who knows - maybe you'll even catch a glimpse of the First Family!

How to get a tour: Submit a request through your Member of Congress at least 21 days in advance (first come first serve basis)

Cost: FREE

Hours: 7:30 a.m. to 11:30 a.m. Tuesday through Thursday, 7:30 a.m. to 1:30 p.m. Fridays and Saturdays

Security Measures:

  • The following are prohibited:
    • Video Recorders
    • Handbags, book bags, backpacks or purses
    • Food or beverages, tobacco products, personal grooming items (i.e. makeup, lotion, etc.)
    • Strollers
    • Any pointed objects
    • Aerosol containers
    • Weapons
  • No flash photography
  • No use of cellphones
  • Need photo ID

Address: 1600 Pennsylvania Ave NW, Washington, DC 20500
