Albert Einstein Memorial

Remember one of the world's greatest scientists of all time at the Albert Einstein Memorial! Admire the scientist and the beautiful bronze and granite used to create his memorial.

Cost: FREE

Hours: 24 hours daily

Address: 2101 Constitution Ave NW, Washington, DC 20418

More Information Here

American Veterans Disabled for Life Memorial

Pay respects to the veterans who sacrificed almost everything for the nation by visiting the American Veterans Disabled for Life Memorial. Learn about the courage and loyalty it takes to fight for the U.S., and give tribute to both the hidden and visual disabilities of veterans across the nation.

Cost: FREE

Hours: 24 hours daily

Address: 150 Washington Ave SW, Washington, DC 20024

More Information Here

Boy Scouts of America Memorial

Meant to memorialize the men and women who brought scouting to life, stop by the Boy Scouts of America Memorial to pay tribute to the Scouts and their work to help create a better America.

Cost: FREE

Hours: 24 hours daily

Address: Located at The Ellipse

More Information Here

Franklin Delano Roosevelt Memorial

To commemorate one of the most influential presidents in U.S. history, the FDR Memorial displays inspiring quotes regarding one of the most tumultuous times in U.S. history. This secluded yet beautiful place highlights some of the most defining truths of what it means to be an American.

Cost: FREE

Hours: Open 24 hours daily

Address: 1850 West Basin Dr SW, Washington, DC 20242

More Information Here

James Buchanan Memorial

Commemorating the nation's 15th president, the James Buchanan memorial is known for its controversy over its construction. Learn about this strife and the struggles of one of our great presidents by visiting the memorial.

Cost: FREE

Hours: 6 a.m. to 9 p.m. daily

Address: Address: Washington, DC 20009

More Information Here

Korean War Veterans Memorial

Honoring one of the most hard fought wars in American history, the Korean War Veterans Memorial displays: 19 stainless steel statues, a mural wall designed by Louis Nelson that displays over 2,400 photographs from the war, the Pool of Remembrance, as well as the United Nations Wall that lists the 22 nations that contributed troops to the United Nations efforts in the war.

Cost: FREE

Hours: 6 a.m. to 11PM Monday-Saturday, Closed Sunday

Address: 900 Ohio Dr SW, Washington, DC 20024

More Information Here

Lincoln Memorial

Sometimes everyone needs a moment of pure calm. Achieve this by stopping by the Lincoln Memorial and admire Abraham Lincoln who helped our country through rough times. Right afterwards, go to the Lincoln Memorial Reflecting Pool, right across from the Lincoln Memorial, where you can appreciate the serene beauty of the water before returning to your busy schedule.

Cost: FREE

Hours: 24 hours Monday-Saturday, 6 a.m. to 5 p.m. Sunday

Address: 2 Lincoln Memorial Cir NW, Washington, DC 20037

More Information Here

Martin Luther King Jr. Memorial

In honor of one of the greatest Civil Rights activists, the MLK Memorial was built to keep Dr. King's work and spirit alive to serve as a beacon of inspiration and education for people of all ages to enjoy and enlighten.

Cost: FREE

Hours: 24 hours daily

Address: 1964 Independence Ave SW, Washington, DC 20024

More Information Here

Peace Monument

Meant to commemorate the naval deaths during the Civil War, the Peace Monument stands for the victory, grief, and history of the United States. Remember those who lost their lives for peace by dropping by the monument, and enjoy the beautiful sculpture.

Cost: FREE

Hours: 24 hours daily

Address: Pennsylvania Avenue and First Street, N.W., Washington D.C.

More Information Here

Theodore Roosevelt Memorial

Stop by the Theodore Roosevelt Island to glimpse the Theodore Roosevelt Memorial. Here, you can learn about the nation's 26th president and pay respects to the work he has done.

Cost: FREE

Hours: 6 a.m. to 10 p.m. daily

Address: Located on Theodore Roosevelt Island

More Information Here

Thomas Jefferson Memorial

Well-known for his Declaration of Independence, the document that essentially kicked off the Revolutionary War, Thomas Jefferson was an extremely influential president. Stop by the Thomas Jefferson Memorial to admire his taste in architecture and remember our nation's 3rd president.

Cost: FREE

Hours: 24 hours daily

Address: 701 E Basin Dr SW, Washington, DC 20242

More Information Here

Titanic Memorial

Most children are taught about the fateful Titanic, sunk in 1914, and the hundreds who died. Memorialize those who lost their lives by visiting the Titanic Memorial, commemorating the unlucky ship.

Cost: FREE

Hours: 24 hours daily

Address: Fourth and P Sts., SW, Washington, D.C.

More Information Here

Vietnam Veterans Memorial

Interested in learning about US military involvement? Visit the Vietnam Veterans Memorial in the National Mall. It includes the names of the people who dedicated their lives to the cause, and memorializes the veterans who took part in the war.

Cost: FREE

Hours: 24 hours daily

Address: 5 Henry Bacon Dr NW, Washington, DC 20245

More Information Here

Vietnam Women's Memorial

Looking back at the many military efforts the U.S. has been a part of over the years, one mostly remembers the men and their fight for freedom. Remember those who are often forgotten by dropping by the Vietnam Women's Memorial, and commemorate the women who played a significant role in the cause.

Cost: FREE

Hours: 24 hours daily

Address: 5 Henry Bacon Dr SW, Washington, DC 20007

More Information Here

Washington Monument

One of the most iconic landmarks of Washington, D.C., the Washington Monument memorializes the nation's first president, George Washington. Stop by and take a quick tour, or simply snap a few pictures as you commemorate one of our Founding Fathers.

Cost: Advanced tickets are $1.50, but same-day pick up tickets are FREE and distributed on a first-come, first-served basis.

Hours: 9 a.m. to 10 p.m. daily

Address: 2 15th St NW, Washington, DC 20024

More Information Here
