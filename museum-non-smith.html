African American Civil War Memorial Museum

You may have learned about the Civil War in school, but textbooks usually gloss over the sacrifices that soldiers made - specifically, African American soldiers. Explore their story by stopping by the African American Civil War Memorial Museum, and learn about the efforts they took to keep the U.S. united.

Cost: FREE

Hours:

  • 10 a.m. to 6:30 p.m. Tuesday - Friday
  • 10 a.m. to 4 p.m. Saturday
  • 12 noon to 4 p.m. Sunday
  • 10 a.m. to 5 p.m. Monday

Policies: United States Park Police are on duty 24 hours a day

Address: 1925 Vermont Ave NW, Washington, DC 20001

More Information Here

Ford's Theater

One of the greatest presidents in history was tragically murdered, and this theater is the murder site. Visit the building where President Abraham Lincoln was assassinated to experience a whirlwind of history. Learn about the Civil War, Lincoln's presidency, and his tragic death all at the Ford's Theater Museum.

Cost: $3 (reserve in advance to guarantee tickets) to tour the entire site

Hours: Check the Ford's Theater website for tour times and showtimes

Policies: The following are prohibited:

  • Tripods
  • Monopods
  • Guns
  • Weapons and toy weapons
  • Knives and pen knives
  • Pets
  • Food and drink (bottled water is permitted)
  • Scissors
  • Aerosol cans
  • Tools
  • Smoking
  • Placards, signs, or banners

Address: 511 10th St NW, Washington, DC 20004

More Information Here

Frederick Douglass National Historic Site

Cost:

  • Entry: FREE
  • Tickets for historic house tour: $1 per person (reserve in advance)

Hours: 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. daily

Policies: N/A

Address: 1411 W St SE, Washington, DC 20020

More Information Here

Newseum

Want to become a reporter someday? Visit the Newseum, where one can experience the life of a reporter firsthand, and explore the history of news and its impact on US history. It contains exhibits such as the NBC News Interactive Room, where visitors experience the actions of a reporter firsthand, and the Berlin Wall Gallery, which displays real life pieces of the Berlin Wall.

Cost:

  • $24.95 + tax for adults
  • $19.95 + tax for seniors
  • $14.95 + tax for youth
  • FREE for children

Hours: 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. Monday-Saturday, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m. Sunday

Policies:

  • Photography without flash and digital filming with smartphones or tablets are encouraged in most parts of the Newseum. Please make sure the flash is turned off when taking any photos to protect the artifacts. Photography and filming of any kind are not allowed in the News Corp. News History Gallery and Newseum theaters. When sharing your photos online, be sure to include #newseum.
  • Only service animals are allowed inside the building.
  • Smoking weapons, and outside food and drink are prohibited.
  • Posters, signs or placards with attached handles and/or larger than 11" x 15" are not permitted. Banners of any size are prohibited without prior approval from the Newseum. Solicitation is strictly prohibited

Address: 555 Pennsylvania Ave NW, Washington, DC 20001

More Information Here
