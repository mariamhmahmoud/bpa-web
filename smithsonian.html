Security Measures for all Smithsonian Buildings (if there are security measures for specific museums, they will be listed under that museum below):

  • Do not bring large daypacks, backpacks, or luggage into the museums, as they will be subject to a lengthy search in each building entered. If possible, leave such items on the tour or school bus or in a hotel room.
  • No accommodations are available for eating bag lunches within the museums except in the Kogod Courtyard of the Reynolds Center for American Art and Portraiture.
  • Do not bring any sharp items into the museums such as knives (including pocket or "Swiss Army"-style knives), screwdrivers, scissors, etc.
  • Other prohibited items include firearms, pepper spray, and Mace.

African-American History and Culture Museum

Documenting the lives of African-Americans from decades ago until today, the African-American History and Culture Museum teaches its visitors about the African-American experience, how being African-American impacts their lives, and how they have helped to shape our nation.

Cost: FREE, but must have timed passes except on walk-up days

Hours: 10 a.m. to 5:30 p.m. everyday, closed on Christmas day

Security Measures: Visitors must go through a metal detector upon entering and all bags will be x-ray checked.

Address: 15th St. and Constitution Ave., NW Washington, DC

More Information Here

African Art Museum

Devoted to the preservation of African Art from all 54 countries in Africa, the African Art Museum collects, exhibits, conserves, and studies the arts of Africa. Displayed are some of the finest examples of traditional and contemporary art for all viewers to absorb and appreciate.

Cost: FREE

Hours: 10 a.m. to 5:30 p.m. daily (open until 7:30 p.m. during extended hours), closed on Christmas day

Address: 950 Independence Avenue, SW Washington, DC

More Information Here

Air and Space Museum

Surround yourself with the some of the countries greatest aircraft, spacecraft, missiles, rockets, and other flight-related artifacts. While here, visitors can go the planetarium and IMAX theater for extraordinary escapes, too.

Cost: FREE

Hours: 10 a.m. to 5:30 p.m. daily, closed on Christmas day

Security Measures: Visitors must go through a metal detector upon entering and all bags will be x-ray checked.

Address:Independence Avenue and 6th Street, SW Washington, DC

More Information Here

American History Museum

If you are looking to learn about the scientific, cultural, social, technological, and political development of the United States from colonial times to present day, the American History Museum is right for you. Observe collections dating back hundreds of years that contain everything from the original Star-Spangled Banner document to Dorothy's ruby slippers from the Wizard of Oz.

Cost: FREE

Hours: 10 a.m. to 5:30 p.m. daily (open until 7:30 p.m. during extended hours), closed on Christmas day

Security Measures: Visitors must go through a metal detector upon entering and all bags will be hand checked.

Address: 14th Street and Constitution Avenue, NW Washington, DC

More Information Here

American Indian Museum

Designed in collaborations with Native tribes and communities, the American Indian Museum displays Native objects, photographs, and media that teach its visitors all about the history and spirit of Native America.

Cost: FREE

Hours: 10 a.m. to 5:30 p.m. daily, closed on Christmas day

Security Measures: Visitors must go through a metal detector upon entering and all bags will be hand checked.

Address: 4th Street and Independence Avenue, SW Washington, DC

More Information Here

Anacostia Community Museum

Getting its name from its location in the Anacostia neighborhood, the Anacostia Community Museum explores, documents, and analyzes the impact of historical and current social issues on urban societies.

Cost: FREE

Hours: 10 a.m. to 5 p.m. daily, closed on Christmas day

Address: 1901 Fort Place SE Washington, DC

More Information Here

Archives of American Art

The Archives of American Art is the world's largest (and continually growing) collection of papers and primary records of the visual arts in America.

Cost: FREE

Hours: 11:30 a.m. to 7 p.m. daily, closed on Christmas day

Address: 8th and F Streets, NW Washington, DC

More Information Here

Freer Gallery of Art

One of two Smithsonian Museums dedicated to Asian art, the Freer Gallery displays Chinese paintings, Indian sculpture, Islamic painting and metalware, Korean ceramics, and so much more.

Cost: FREE

Hours: 10 a.m. to 5:30 p.m. daily, closed on Christmas day

Address: Jefferson Drive and 12th Street, SW Washington, DC

More Information Here

Hirshhorn Museum

This museum features international modern and contemporary art in the Gordon Bunshaft designed cylindrical building, adjoining plaza, and sunken sculpture garden.

Cost: FREE

Hours:

  • Museum: 10 a.m. to 5:30 p.m. daily
  • Plaza: 7:30 a.m. to 5:30 p.m. daily
  • Sculpture Garden: 7:30 a.m. to dusk daily
  • All closed on Christmas day

Address: Independence Avenue at 7th Street, SW Washington, DC

More Information Here

National Zoo

Home to over 1,500 animals and 300 different species, the National Zoo teaches its visitors about animals across the globe and gives everyone the opportunity to be within feet of them.

Cost: FREE

Hours:

  • March 15-September: 9 a.m. to 6 p.m. daily (Grounds: 8 a.m. to 7 p.m.)
  • October-March 14: 9 a.m. to 4 p.m. daily (Grounds: 8 a.m. to 5 p.m.)
  • Always closed on Christmas day and during bad weather

Address: 3001 Connecticut Ave., NW Washington, DC

More Information Here

Natural History Museum

The Natural History Museum is dedicated to understanding the world we live in and what our role is within it. Learn about our world's creation and explore the diversity of live through exhibitions, activities, collections, and research that happens in the lab and field.

Cost: FREE

Hours: 10 a.m. to 5:30 p.m. daily (open until 7:30 p.m. during extended hours), closed on Christmas day

Security Measures: Visitors must go through a metal detector upon entering and all bags will be hand checked.

Address: 10th Street and Constitution Avenue, NW Washington, DC

More Information Here

Portrait Gallery

Whether you're looking to learn about poets, presidents, actors, visionaries, villains, or anyone in between, the Portrait Gallery is the right place for you. The collection of portraits in this gallery along with stories and biographies paint the picture of America from precolonial times until now.

Cost: FREE

Hours: 11:30 a.m. to 7 p.m. daily, closed on Christmas day

Address: 8th and F Streets, NW Washington, DC

More Information Here

Postal Museum

Located in the historic D.C. City Post Office, this museum displays the largest collection of stamps and postal-related materials such as mail trucks, card, letters, and materials that came before the use of stamps. Visitors can walk along an old post road, ride with mail in a stagecoach, browse a small post office from the early 20th century, and start their very own stamp collection with free stamps.

Cost: FREE

Hours: 10 a.m. to 5:30 p.m. daily, closed on Christmas day

Security Measures: Visitors must go through a metal detector upon entering and all bags will be x-ray checked.

Address: 2 Massachusetts Avenue, NE Washington, DC

More Information Here

Renwick Gallery

Dedicated to exhibiting American contemporary craft, the Renwick Gallery celebrates creators taking innovative approaches to their works. The building housing this gallery was the first building in the United States built specifically to be an art museum.

Cost: FREE

Hours: 10 a.m. to 5:30 p.m. daily, closed on Christmas day

Address: 1661 Pennsylvania Avenue, NW Washington, DC

More Information Here

Sackler Gallery

The second gallery dedicated to Asian art, the Sackler Gallery houses both permanent and temporary pieces from ancient times to the present. It includes some of the most important Chinese jades and bronzes, modern Japanese ceramics, and features lectures, concerts, films, and podcasts that all visitors are welcome to attend.

Cost: FREE

Hours: 10 a.m. to 5:30 p.m. daily, closed on Christmas day

Address: 1050 Independence Avenue, SW Washington, DC

More Information Here

Smithsonian Institution Building (The Castle)

The Castle is the signature Smithsonian building and home to the visitor center. While here, visitors can learn about what each museum has to offer, tour the Castle, and consult with experts about what to see and do.

Cost: FREE

Hours: 8:30 a.m. to 5:30 p.m. daily, closed on Christmas day

Address: 1000 Jefferson Drive, SW Washington, DC

More Information Here
