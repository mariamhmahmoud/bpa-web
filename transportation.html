Uber

With Uber, you can choose the size of your car, pay through the Uber app, and see the wait time, cost, and travel time estimate before you decide to get the Uber.

Cost: Depends on time in the vehicle, distance, surge variable (if demand is greater than the number of drivers available, the cost increases), and tipping.

Hours of Operation: 24 hours daily

Takes you directly to your destination without stops: Yes

Schedule: You schedule a ride anytime you need it.

Walking

Since the sidewalks stretch all across the city, walking is a great transportation option. Also, walking is the only option to see the National Mall and monuments unless you need a wheelchair. There is no cost involved, no wait time, and you get exercise.

Cost: FREE

Hours of Operation: 24 hours daily

Takes you directly to your destination without stops: Yes (that is, unless you stop along the way)

Schedule: You can walk anytime!



Taxi

With the highest taxi to citizen ratio in the country, it's pretty easy to find a taxi in Washington D.C. All taxis accept cash, credit, or debit, making them extremely convenient. Also, an app called "DC Taxi Rider" is available on iPhones and Androids and allows you to choose the size of your taxi and see a cost and time estimate. Overall, taxis are a great way to get around the city.

Cost:

  • Minimum fare: $3.25
  • Mileage charge: $0.27 per 1/8 mile
  • Additional passenger: $1
  • Possible luggage charge of $0.50 per item in the trunk
  • Each trip originating at the Reagan National Airport has a surcharge of $2.50.
  • Phone dispatch fee: $2.00
  • Dismissal without use charge: $1.50

Hours of Operation: 24 hours daily

Takes you directly to your destination without stops: Yes

Schedule: You can hail a taxi anytime you see one by sticking your arm out or schedule a taxi to pick you up up by phone anytime.

More Information Here

Photo Credit: "Taxi." Meadows Field Airport, www.meadowsfield.com/taxis/.



Bike and Roll DC

If you're looking to get some exercise and have a great time, rent a bike and adventure all around D.C.! Bike and Roll DC offers electric bikes, comfort hybrids, performance bikes, road bikes, tandem bikes, kids' bikes, trailer tandems, trailers/burleys, and manual wheelchairs.

Cost:

  • 2 hour minimum: $40
  • Additional $20/hour
  • $100 for full day
  • Minimum age of 16
  • 2 hour minimum: $16
  • Additional $8/hour
  • Maximum day: $40
  • $50 if rented for 24 hours
  • 2 hour minimum: $30
  • Additional $15/hour
  • Maximum day: $75
  • $85 if rented for 24 hours
  • 2 hour minimum: $10
  • Additional $5/hour
  • Maximum day: $25
  • $35 if rented for 24 hours

Hours of Operation:

  • 955 L'Enfant Plaza SW North Building Washington, DC 20024-2119 location: 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. daily
  • 50 Massachusetts Ave NE Washington, DC 20002 location: 8 a.m. to 5 p.m. Monday-Saturday, Closed Sunday

Takes you directly to your destination without stops: Yes (that is, unless you stop along the way)

Schedule: You can rent a bike anytime during these locations' hours of operation.

More Information Here

Capital Bikeshare

With the ability to pick up a bike anywhere as long as you have a pass or membership, Capital Bikeshare is an extremely convenient way of traveling. It is also extremely affordable and allows you to exercise while sightseeing!

Cost:

  • Single trip (under 30 minutes): $2
  • 24 hour pass: $8
  • 3-day pass: $17

Hours of Operation: 24 hours daily

Takes you directly to your destination without stops: Yes (that is, unless you stop along the way)

Schedule: You can ride anytime!

DC Metro Trains

A cost-effective way to get around the city, DC Metro Trains give riders the opportunity to ditch their cars or bikes and get around the city by subway. While it does take longer to get to your destination than many other methods, riding the subway is a unique way to get around.

Cost:

  • During peak hours: Between $2.25 and $6.00 per trip
  • Off-peak hours: Between $1.85 and $3.85 per trip
  • Must pay via SmarTrip card

Hours of Operation:

  • 5 a.m. to 11:30 p.m. Monday-Thursday
  • 5 a.m. to 1 p.m. Friday
  • 7 a.m. to 1 a.m. Saturday
  • 8 a.m. to 11 p.m. Sunday

Takes you directly to your destination without stops: No

Schedule: Visit the Washington Metropolitan Area Transit Authority website to see all train schedules and locations.

More Information Here

Metrobus

Providing more than 400,000 trips each day throughout the District of Columbia, Maryland, and Virginia, Metrobus is one of the more common ways of getting around the D.C. area.

Cost:

  • During peak hours: Between $2.25 and $6.00 per trip
  • Off-peak hours: Between $1.85 and $3.85 per trip
  • Must pay via SmarTrip card

Hours of Operation:

  • 5 a.m. to 11:30 p.m. Monday-Thursday
  • 5 a.m. to 1 p.m. Friday
  • 7 a.m. to 1 a.m. Saturday
  • 8 a.m. to 11 p.m. Sunday

Takes you directly to your destination without stops: No

Schedule: Visit the Washington Metropolitan Area Transit Authority website to see all train schedules and locations.

More Information Here

DC Circulator

One of few free ways to get around D.C., DC Circulator offers rides over many routes throughout the city and is extremely cost-effective.

Cost: FREE

Hours of Operation:

  • Dupont Circle-Georgetown-Rosslyn, Georgetown-Union Station, Woodley Park-Adams Morgan-McPherson Square Metro routes
    • 6 a.m. to 12 midnight Monday-Thursday
    • 6 am. to 3 a.m. Friday
    • 7 a.m. to 3 a.m. Saturday
    • 7 a.m. to 12 midnight Sunday
  • Eastern Market-L'Enfant Plaza and Congress Heights-Union Station route
    • 6 a.m. to 9.p.m. Monday-Friday
    • 7 a.m. to 9 p.m. Saturday-Sunday
  • National Mall (October-March) route
    • 7 a.m. to 7 p.m. Monday-Friday
    • 9 a.m. to 7 p.m. Saturday-Sunday
  • National Mall (April-September) route
    • 7 a.m. to 8 p.m. Monday-Friday
    • 9 a.m. to 8 p.m. Saturday-Sunday

Takes you directly to your destination without stops: Occasional unscheduled stops are made, but the buses generally do not make stops.

Schedule: Buses run every ten minutes during the hours of operation along the routes listed above.

More Information Here

Photo Credit: "DC Circulator Bus." DC Circulator, www.dccirculator.com.



DC Streetcar

One of the cheapest ways of getting around the city that usually takes you to your destination without stopping, DC Streetcar is a fast, great way to go short distances.

Cost:

  • $1 regular fare
  • $0.50 for seniors and disabled
  • Payment options include cash and SmarTrip Card

Hours of Operation:

  • 6 a.m. to 12 midnight Monday-Thursday
  • 6 a.m. to 2 a.m. Friday
  • 8 a.m. to 2 a.m. Saturday
  • 8 a.m. to 10 p.m. Sunday and holidays

Takes you directly to your destination without stops: No

Schedule: Visit the DC Streetcar website to see the transit map and track a streetcar. Cars typically arrive at each stop in 12-15 minutes.
